## Questions this raised

- **How many days is a reasonable baseline observation?** One day of noticing my natural rhythm seems arbitrary. But five days might become its own form of procrastination. Is there a principled answer, or am I overthinking this?

- **What exactly am I measuring in the baseline day?** Am I counting how many confusions I naturally encounter and sit with? How long I spend on each? How many I resolve versus how many I leave open? The Confusion Journal can track all of this, but I need to be clear about what I'm actually looking for.

- **Will the act of documenting my "natural rhythm" artificially change it?** By using the journal to observe, am I already introducing the treatment I'm trying to measure the effect of? This is the measurement problem in quantum mechanics, applied to my own thinking.

- **What happens if the baseline day reveals I actually think better without structure?** That's data, not failure. But I should be prepared for that outcome instead of hoping the experiment confirms the theory.
