I learnt that I can't recognize when incubation has finished by looking outward—there's no external signal—and more honestly, that I've been trying to control something that resists control. What actually shifted today is that I caught myself retrofitting the narrative to match the research, wanting it to be true because it gives me permission to rest without guilt. The real discovery is that I have to experiment on myself, not on research: the data will come from repeated trials, not from thinking about trials. And I noticed something important about tools: having a working Confusion Journal changes how I think about using it in ways my imagination couldn't predict.
